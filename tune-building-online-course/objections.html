I don't believe in online lessons

Don't believe in online bagpipe lessons? You're not alone! Many share the same doubt about the efficacy of learning through a screen.

While the traditional "master-to-apprentice" approach shines in its personal touch, it's not the only path to mastery. Here at our school, we've built a unique alternative, one that embraces innovation and accessibility.

Let me take you on a brief journey:

Back in 2011, while on my honeymoon in Playa del Carmen, a thought struck me: What if we brought the magic of bagpipe camps online? Imagine not just a week of learning, but ongoing support, a community growing together, and world-class instruction accessible from home.

The reality may present challenges — and I acknowledge that there are skills which require hands-on guidance. However, the benefits of our online approach far outweigh these limitations:

  • **World-class teaching** from the comfort of your home.
  • No travel hassle, no gas money, and importantly, no health risks.
  • Access to a large, vibrant community of fellow pipers.
  • Hundreds of weekly learning opportunities—far beyond the one a year at camps.
  • An endless archive of recorded classes and valuable resources.

Many have transformed from novices to advanced pipers through our online platform, and with the right mindset, you can too!

I don't have the time or energy to practice

I hear you! Life can be overwhelming, and fitting in practice often falls to the wayside. But the truth is, most people don’t actually lack time; they simply need results that stimulate their motivation.

Think about it: we all carve out time for Netflix, family, and hobbies. If only bagpiping could deliver tangible rewards, it wouldn’t feel like a chore.

Now, imagine if every practice minute truly advanced your skills. What if, just one month from now, you were not only sounding better but also playing confidently and enjoying your music again?

Here’s our promise: with our proven system, it will click. And when it does, you won’t need to find time—you’ll want to make it.
